About Barnaby Snipe

Barnaby Snipe writes dry, socially observant comic fiction. His work is drawn to island life, human appetite, local friction, and the ways communities reveal themselves under pressure.

Barnaby Snipe writes dry, socially observant comic fiction. His work is drawn to place, appetite, class friction, local politics, and the way communities behave when pressure unsettles their ordinary routines. Across his fiction, he is interested in people trying to preserve their dignity while gossip spreads, shortages bite, and private wants become public weather.

His stories are rooted in character, community, and the practical details of lived life. Rather than broad farce, the comedy comes from people under pressure: the things they want, the things they hide, the stories they tell, and the ways a place can begin to act like a single nervous system.

He is the author of Herb Aplenty.

  • Writing signature Dry social comedy rather than broad farce
  • Recurring interests Place, appetite, class friction, gossip, and local pressure
  • Comic method Community behaviour, practical detail, and people trying to keep their dignity
  • Current book Herb Aplenty

Current title

Herb Aplenty

A comic novel of island chaos, shortage and temptation.

When an island runs dry, private habits turn into public weather. Rumour spreads, dignity frays, outsiders complicate matters, and a community that likes to think it knows itself begins to show its workings. Herb Aplenty is a comic novel of shortage, temptation, local friction, and the trouble people make for themselves when relief sits just out of reach.
