About

Comedy that comes from people, place, and pressure.

Rather than broad farce, the comedy here comes from appetite, manners, local frictions, and the hard work people do to keep their private wants from becoming public weather.

Barnaby Snipe writes dry, socially observant comic fiction. His work is drawn to place, appetite, class friction, local politics, and the way communities behave when pressure unsettles their ordinary routines. Across his fiction, he is interested in people trying to preserve their dignity while gossip spreads, shortages bite, and private wants become public weather.

His stories are rooted in character, community, and the practical details of lived life. Rather than broad farce, the comedy comes from people under pressure: the things they want, the things they hide, the stories they tell, and the ways a place can begin to act like a single nervous system.
