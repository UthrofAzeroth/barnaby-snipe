About

Comedy built from people, place, and social pressure.

Rather than broad farce, the comedy here comes from appetite, manners, local frictions, and the effort people make to keep private wants from becoming public business.

Barnaby Snipe writes dry, socially observant comic fiction. His work is drawn to place, appetite, class friction, local politics, and the way communities behave when pressure unsettles their ordinary routines.

His stories are rooted in character, community, and the practical details of lived life. Harbour routines, stock lists, storage rooms, errands, queues, and overheard talk all do real comic work.
